## Summary

This category compares the operating and economic architecture behind common technology business models.

## What's in this category

- **[<span id="managed-services">Managed Services</span>](/wiki/business-models/managed-services)**: Combine expertise, people, and process into a repeatable customer outcome.
- **[<span id="api-as-a-product">API-as-a-Product</span>](/wiki/business-models/api-as-a-product)**: Build and sell a developer-facing capability as an integrated product.
- **[<span id="hardware-as-a-service">Hardware-as-a-Service</span>](/wiki/business-models/hardware-as-a-service)**: Package equipment, software, service, and financing into a recurring offer.
- **[<span id="everything-as-a-service">Everything-as-a-Service (XaaS)</span>](/wiki/business-models/everything-as-a-service)**: Apply recurring service logic across software, infrastructure, and connected products.
- **[<span id="marketplace-model">Marketplace Model</span>](/wiki/business-models/marketplace-model)**: Match supply and demand, then monetize liquidity and trust.
- **[<span id="razor-and-blades">Razor-and-Blades</span>](/wiki/business-models/razor-and-blades)**: Use an installed base to support recurring consumables or usage revenue.
- **[<span id="vertical-saas">Vertical SaaS</span>](/wiki/business-models/vertical-saas)**: Build deep workflow software for a specific industry.
- **[<span id="open-source-business-models">Open-Source Business Models</span>](/wiki/business-models/open-source-business-models)**: Convert adoption and community into durable commercial value.
- **[<span id="ai-native-business-models">AI-Native Business Models</span>](/wiki/business-models/ai-native-business-models)**: Align automation, compute cost, and customer outcomes.
- **[<span id="saas">SaaS</span>](/wiki/business-models/saas)**: Understand recurring software revenue, retention, and operating leverage.
- **[<span id="platform-business-model">Platform vs. Pipeline</span>](/wiki/business-models/platform-business-model)**: Compare linear value creation with participant ecosystems.
- **[<span id="direct-to-consumer">Direct-to-Consumer (D2C)</span>](/wiki/business-models/direct-to-consumer)**: Own the customer relationship and the economics of reaching and serving it.
- **[<span id="advertising-model">Advertising Model</span>](/wiki/business-models/advertising-model)**: Monetize attention, intent, or access to an audience.

## How to use this

Start with the delivery model closest to the current offer, then compare adjacent models for margin, scale, and defensibility trade-offs.
