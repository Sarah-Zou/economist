## Summary

Monetization models are less about "what price" and more about **what you charge for**. This category helps you pick the right **revenue engine** (subscription, seats, usage, transactions, outcomes), choose a **value metric** customers accept, and decide when **freemium**, **hybrid**, or **credits/drawdown** structures balance adoption, predictability, and expansion.

## What's in this category

- **[<span id="monetization-model">Monetization model</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/monetization-model)**: Your revenue architecture: who pays, for what, and how revenue scales as customers succeed.
- **[<span id="pricing-metric-value-metric">Pricing metric / value metric</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/pricing-metric-value-metric)**: The single unit you bill on (seat, GB, transaction, outcome) that should track customer value and your cost-to-serve.
- **[<span id="subscription-model">Subscription model</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/subscription-model)**: Charge a recurring fee for ongoing access when value is continuous and usage is frequent enough to justify "always on."
- **[<span id="seat-based-pricing">Seat-based pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/seat-based-pricing)**: A subscription priced by users/licenses when value scales with headcount and buyers want budget predictability.
- **[<span id="usage-based-pricing">Usage-based pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing)**: Charge by measured consumption (API calls, tokens, GB, minutes) when usage is the clearest proxy for value and costs scale with use.
- **[<span id="transaction-based-pricing">Transaction-based pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/transaction-based-pricing)**: Charge per discrete value event (for example, per payment, booking, or shipment) when each transaction is auditable and outcome-proximate.
- **[<span id="outcome-performance-based-pricing">Outcome / performance-based pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/outcome-performance-based-pricing)**: Tie price to results (revenue uplift, savings, conversions) when outcomes are measurable, attributable, and auditable.
- **[<span id="freemium-model">Freemium model</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/freemium-model)**: Use a free tier as an acquisition channel, then convert users to paid tiers when marginal cost is low and growth loops exist.
- **[<span id="hybrid-pricing">Hybrid pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing)**: Combine a fixed base (subscription/seat) with a variable component (usage/transactions/outcomes) to balance predictability and upside.
- **[<span id="credits-drawdown-model">Credits / drawdown model</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/credits-drawdown-model)**: Customers prepay a wallet of credits and consume that balance as usage occurs, blending budget certainty with flexible consumption.

## Which model fits your product?

| Your product's reality | Best-fit model(s) | The page to read |
|------------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Value scales with how many people use it (collaboration, CRM) | Subscription per seat | [Seat-based pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/seat-based-pricing) |
| Continuous value, stable usage, recurring need | Flat or tiered recurring fee | [Subscription model](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/subscription-model) |
| Usage varies 10× across customers; costs scale with use (API, data, AI) | Consumption pricing, usually with a base | [Usage-based pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing) |
| Value arrives in discrete, countable events (payments, bookings) | Per-event pricing | [Transaction-based pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/transaction-based-pricing) |
| The result is measurable, attributable, and auditable | Gainshare / success fee | [Outcome-based pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/outcome-performance-based-pricing) |
| Buyers need budget certainty AND you need upside | Base + variable | [Hybrid pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) |
| Procurement wants committed spend; usage is spiky | Prepaid wallet | [Credits / drawdown](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/credits-drawdown-model) |
| Near-zero marginal cost, viral loops, huge market | Free tier feeding paid | [Freemium model](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/freemium-model) |
| You're not sure what unit to bill on at all | Start here first | [Pricing metric / value metric](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/pricing-metric-value-metric) |

## Insights

### Foundation Insight

Your [Monetization model](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/monetization-model) is the broad canvas; your [Pricing metric](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/pricing-metric-value-metric) is the specific brush. If you get the model right but the metric wrong, you will face high churn. The perfect pricing metric scales in lockstep with the customer's perceived value.

### Revenue Engines Insight
Most software companies operate on a [Subscription Model](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/subscription-model). Historically, the default metric for these subscriptions was [Seat-Based Pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/seat-based-pricing). It's easy to build and highly predictable for both the founder and the buyer. However, it creates friction. Customers share logins to avoid paying for extra seats, which stops your product from spreading virally through their organization. To solve this, the industry is shifting toward [Usage-Based Pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing) and [Transaction-Based Pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/transaction-based-pricing). These models remove user-count friction and align better with realized value—customers pay when usage or transactions happen. The tradeoff is predictability: variable billing can make revenue forecasting difficult for early-stage startups and can scare away enterprise buyers who need budget certainty. [Outcome-Based Pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/outcome-performance-based-pricing) is the holy grail of value alignment, but it is rarely used because proving attribution (e.g., "Did our software actually generate that $1M, or was it your sales team?") is notoriously difficult. 

### Advanced & GTM Insight

[Hybrid pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) charges a platform fee to cover fixed costs and secure predictable MRR, then adds usage-based components to capture upside as clients scale. The [Credits / drawdown model](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/credits-drawdown-model) is a practical predictability layer for variable businesses: customers pre-commit budget and consume it over time. [Freemium](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/freemium-model) sits outside the revenue debate—it feeds your revenue engines by letting users experience value risk-free before hitting the paywall.

## How to use this

### Start simple

Choose 1 primary model and 1 value metric a customer can repeat back; complexity can come later.

### The "Aha!" Metric Audit

Audit your current [pricing metric](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/pricing-metric-value-metric) against your product's "Aha!" moment.

### Match pricing to go-to-market

PLG/self-serve often pairs with [usage-based](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing), [transaction-based](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/transaction-based-pricing), or [freemium](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/freemium-model) (low friction), while enterprise buyers tend to prefer seats and/or a [hybrid](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) structure, often with [credits / drawdown](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/credits-drawdown-model), for budget predictability.

### Protect unit economics early

If marginal costs scale, avoid pure flat pricing; add [usage](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing) or [transaction](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/transaction-based-pricing) components, and consider [credits / drawdown](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/credits-drawdown-model) or a [hybrid](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) base to prevent margin blow-ups at high volume.

### Use pricing to drive expansion

Move from [seats](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/seat-based-pricing) to [usage](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing), [transaction-based](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/transaction-based-pricing), or [hybrid](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) to uncap NRR as customers grow, and use [credits / drawdown](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/credits-drawdown-model) to make that expansion budgetable for procurement-heavy buyers.
