## Summary

Monetization models are less about "what price" and more about **what you charge for**. This category helps you pick the right **revenue engine** (subscription, seats, usage, outcomes), choose a **value metric** customers accept, and decide when **freemium** or **hybrid** structures balance adoption, predictability, and expansion.

## What's in this category

- **[<span id="monetization-model">Monetization model</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/monetization-model)**: Your revenue architecture: who pays, for what, and how revenue scales as customers succeed.
- **[<span id="pricing-metric-value-metric">Pricing metric / value metric</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/pricing-metric-value-metric)**: The single unit you bill on (seat, GB, transaction, outcome) that should track customer value and your cost-to-serve.
- **[<span id="subscription-model">Subscription model</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/subscription-model)**: Charge a recurring fee for ongoing access when value is continuous and usage is frequent enough to justify "always on."
- **[<span id="seat-based-pricing">Seat-based pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/seat-based-pricing)**: A subscription priced by users/licenses when value scales with headcount and buyers want budget predictability.
- **[<span id="usage-based-pricing">Usage-based pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing)**: Charge by measured consumption (API calls, tokens, GB, minutes) when usage is the clearest proxy for value and costs scale with use.
- **[<span id="outcome-performance-based-pricing">Outcome / performance-based pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/outcome-performance-based-pricing)**: Tie price to results (revenue uplift, savings, conversions) when outcomes are measurable, attributable, and auditable.
- **[<span id="freemium-model">Freemium model</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/freemium-model)**: Use a free tier as an acquisition channel, then convert users to paid tiers when marginal cost is low and growth loops exist.
- **[<span id="hybrid-pricing">Hybrid pricing</span>](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing)**: Combine a fixed base (subscription/seat) with a variable component (usage/transactions/outcomes) to balance predictability and upside.

## Core Concepts

### Core Architecture

- **Monetization model:** The overarching blueprint for who pays, for what, and how revenue scales.
- **Pricing metric / value metric:** The specific billable unit (seat, GB, transaction, outcome) that should track value and cost-to-serve.

### Revenue Engines

- **Subscription model:** Recurring access pricing for continuous value and stable usage.
- **Seat-based pricing:** Per-user recurring pricing when value scales with headcount and buyers want budget predictability.
- **Usage-based pricing:** Consumption pricing when usage is the clearest value proxy and cost scales with use.
- **Outcome-based pricing:** Value-sharing pricing tied to measurable, attributable results.

### Advanced Execution

- **Hybrid pricing:** Base fee plus variable usage to combine predictable revenue with upside capture.

### Go-To-Market Pricing

- **Freemium model:** A free acquisition tier that feeds paid conversion and expansion when marginal costs are low.

## Insights

### Foundation Insight

Your [Monetization model](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/monetization-model) is the broad canvas; your [Pricing metric](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/pricing-metric-value-metric) is the specific brush. If you get the model right but the metric wrong, you will face high churn. The perfect pricing metric scales in lockstep with the customer's perceived value.

### Revenue Engines Insight
Most software companies operate on a [Subscription Model](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/subscription-model). Historically, the default metric for these subscriptions was [Seat-Based Pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/seat-based-pricing). It's easy to build and highly predictable for both the founder and the buyer. However, it creates friction. Customers share logins to avoid paying for extra seats, which stops your product from spreading virally through their organization. To solve this, the industry is shifting toward [Usage-Based Pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing). This removes the friction of adding new users and aligns perfectly with customer success—if they don't use it, they don't pay. However, UBP makes revenue forecasting incredibly difficult for early-stage startups and can scare away enterprise buyers who need predictable budgets. [Outcome-Based Pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/outcome-performance-based-pricing) is the holy grail of value alignment, but it is rarely used because proving attribution (e.g., "Did our software actually generate that $1M, or was it your sales team?") is notoriously difficult. 

### Advanced & GTM Insight

[Hybrid pricing](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) charges a platform fee to cover fixed costs and secure predictable MRR, then adds usage-based components to capture upside as clients scale. [Freemium](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/freemium-model) sits outside the revenue debate—it feeds your revenue engines by letting users experience value risk-free before hitting the paywall.

## How to use this

### Start simple

Choose 1 primary model and 1 value metric a customer can repeat back; complexity can come later.

### The "Aha!" Metric Audit

Audit your current [pricing metric](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/pricing-metric-value-metric) against your product's "Aha!" moment.

### Match pricing to go-to-market

PLG/self-serve often pairs with [usage-based](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing) or [freemium](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/freemium-model) (low friction), while enterprise buyers tend to prefer seats and/or a [hybrid](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) structure for budget predictability.

### Protect unit economics early

If marginal costs scale, avoid pure flat pricing; add [usage](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing) components or a [hybrid](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) base to prevent margin blow-ups at high usage.

### Use pricing to drive expansion

Move from [seats](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/seat-based-pricing) to [usage](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing)/[hybrid](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/hybrid-pricing) to uncap NRR as customers grow, or use [usage](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/usage-based-pricing)/[outcome-based positioning](/wiki/pricing/models-and-metering/outcome-performance-based-pricing) to reduce buyer risk when competing against expensive legacy seat-based incumbents.
