## Summary

Startup funding is not just cash — it is a choice about claims on the company. This category covers how founders size a round, compare bootstrapping, debt, SAFEs, and priced equity, and understand dilution and control at each stage.

## What's in this category

- **[<span id="how-startup-funding-works">How Startup Funding Works</span>](/fundraising/how-startup-funding-works)**: A founder's guide to funding sources, instruments, round sizing, dilution, and the trade-offs between bootstrapping, debt, SAFEs, and equity.
- **[<span id="pre-seed-seed-series-a">Pre-Seed, Seed, and Series A</span>](/fundraising/pre-seed-seed-series-a)**: Pre-seed, seed, and Series A are market labels for different risk-reduction jobs—not fixed revenue, valuation, or check-size thresholds.
- **[<span id="safe-notes">SAFE Notes</span>](/fundraising/safe-notes)**: A SAFE gives an investor a contractual right to future equity when a defined event occurs; the cap, discount, capitalization definition, and later financing terms decide the shares issued.
- **[<span id="convertible-notes">Convertible Notes</span>](/fundraising/convertible-notes)**: A convertible note is startup debt that usually converts into equity at a later financing; principal, accrued interest, the cap or discount, and the capitalization definition determine the shares issued.
- **[<span id="startup-valuation-methods">Startup Valuation Methods</span>](/fundraising/startup-valuation-methods)**: A startup valuation is a purpose-specific estimate or negotiated price; founders should triangulate market evidence, operating scenarios, financing ownership, and security rights instead of treating one formula as truth.
- **[<span id="dilution-cap-tables">Dilution and Cap Tables</span>](/fundraising/dilution-cap-tables)**: A cap table records who owns which securities; a dilution model shows how financing, options, convertibles, and other issuances change each holder's percentage and economic outcome.
- **[<span id="term-sheets">Term Sheets</span>](/fundraising/term-sheets)**: A venture term sheet summarizes the proposed financing's economics, governance, and process; the headline valuation matters only in combination with dilution, preference, control, and closing conditions.
- **[<span id="liquidation-preferences">Liquidation Preferences</span>](/fundraising/liquidation-preferences)**: A liquidation preference determines who gets paid first in a startup exit; its multiple, participation, seniority, dividends, and conversion terms can matter more than ownership percentages at modest outcomes.
