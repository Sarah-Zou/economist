# EconNova Photography Prompt

Use this as the master prompt for future website and article photography. Replace the bracketed fields while keeping the visual rules consistent.

```text
Use case: photorealistic-natural
Asset type: [website hero / website section photograph / article header / newsletter cover]

Primary request: Create an editorial photograph about [specific idea, decision, or tension]. Show the idea through one concrete, believable scene rather than a literal business metaphor.

Editorial purpose: The image should help a thoughtful reader feel [desired idea or mood] before reading the copy. It must add meaning, not serve as generic decoration.

Scene/backdrop: [one specific real environment]. Keep the setting quiet, contemporary, and naturally imperfect.

Subject: [one primary subject or small group of related objects]. Include only details that support the idea. If documents, charts, notebooks, or screens appear, use abstract marks and shapes with no readable or invented text.

Style/medium: Highly photorealistic natural editorial photography with the restraint of Scandinavian architectural photography and an independent print magazine. Observed rather than staged. Real material texture, subtle wear, gentle film grain, and believable depth of field.

Composition/framing:
- Use a [3:2 / 16:9 / portrait] crop appropriate for [placement].
- Establish one clear focal point and quiet asymmetry.
- For a website image with adjacent copy, reserve clean negative space on the [left/right].
- For an article header, make the subject readable at thumbnail size without adding text to the image.
- Avoid centering everything or filling every part of the frame.

Lighting/mood: Soft natural window or overcast daylight with believable falloff and restrained shadows. Calm, intelligent, warm, and precise—not dramatic, glossy, or cinematic.

Color palette: Warm ivory, pale oak, stone, muted clay, charcoal, and restrained deep navy. Natural color balance with no dominant black, heavy orange grade, or cold blue cast.

Materials/textures: Real paper grain, wood, stone, brushed metal, natural fabric, glass, or architectural surfaces as appropriate. Preserve small imperfections.

Constraints: No text, logos, trademarks, watermark, decorative overlays, fake interface copy, or unnecessary props. People should only appear when the story genuinely requires them; if included, use natural posture, realistic skin texture, and unforced expression.

Avoid: Generic corporate stock photography, handshakes, posed meetings, laptop-and-coffee clichés, staged brainstorming walls, excessive props, perfect symmetry, luxury-office signaling, hyper-sharp CGI polish, artificial bokeh, heavy retouching, or visual metaphors that explain the idea too literally.
```

## Recommended prompt variables

- **Website sections:** name the exact neighboring section and which side needs negative space.
- **Article headers:** provide the article thesis, not just its title, and ask for one concrete visual interpretation.
- **Portraits:** supply the original portrait as the protected identity reference and explicitly prohibit relighting, reshaping, smoothing, or facial reconstruction.
- **Series consistency:** repeat the same lighting, palette, texture, and avoid-list language across every image.
